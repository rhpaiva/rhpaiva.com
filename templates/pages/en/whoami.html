The longer story

In 2001, when I was 16, I got curious to know how internet pages work and began to inform myself about this topic. After figuring out that I needed to learn HTML to create websites, I remembered that I had a small HTML book from an informatic course that I attended when I was 14, so I started stuyind it. It was a very small book but enough to learn what I needed for my first experiments.

There comes IRC

Because of my curiosity for HTML, I started accessing the IRC in order to ask for help in channels like #webmasters (I even became later an OP) in the old network Brasnet. At first I used to access them just to ask for help and didn't interact with the users that much, but as my knowledge got bigger, I started helping out other uses as well. Because of that, I got to meet some guys who started by the same time as me and who turned out to become excellent professionals.

How can I create dynamic stuff?

In the same year of 2001, after the "well succeeded" creation of my first website, which I hosted in a free server, with a "logo" done on WordArt from MS Word and many fancy/exotic javascripts, I realized that HTML was not enough for me. This way I found out that I could use server side programming to include files. That was fantastic! That's when I started studying more and more about doing things on the server and discovered ASP. I bought my first programming book (The ASP Bible with more than 1000 pages) and began reading it through. I was reading that book all day long and, the more I learned, the more I wanted to learn. Programming and watching things happen through code was fascinating!

The start of a journey

In 2002 I took part in a program as intern for Ford Motors Brazil where I worked assisting with office (not the MS one) bureaucracy for other employees and that was my only job unrelated to IT. Because of this job, I had to work during the day and finish high school at night, this way in this year I had to stop studying IT stuff for a while.

In 2003 I got an internship for a small company in the eletrocnics business where I continued the development of their internal ERP made in ASP with MySQL. From that moment on I haven't stopped working with IT and since 2004 I work with PHP.

I've already worked for small, medium and big companies. I've developed intranets for the Brazilian Ministry of Health and Japanese Government entities, ecommerce and institutional websites, a proprietary markup language with parser for templates used in a manager for thematic portals, a system for ticket resolution (helpdesk) in a hosting platform, an ERP for stores and its branches, etc...

Since 2007 I've been working with webstandards and semantic markup and I've been evangelizing those practices in every place and project that I've worked since then.

Curiosity for learning

I'm also interested in Frontend (UX) and its related technologies such as Usability, Accessibility, Wireframes (I use Balsamiq), SEO and Responsive Layouts.

I became a fan of CSS pre-processors (I use LESS), Sublime Text, Emmet and Grunt, but I still like to write plain Javascript (or with JQuery), and I'm not yet convinced that Coffeescript is useful.

I think I still don't know Python, but I'm on my way to achieve it :). I also study Node.js, its ecosystem, and find it really awesome how much Javascript's ecosystem has evolved since I've started learning this language..

So... only IT? What about other things?

Nope... I'm also interested in languages (the human ones), other cultures, playing guitar, football (not the one played with hands), swimming and running (I try to run at least 15 km every week).
